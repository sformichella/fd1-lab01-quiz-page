The Infinitude of Prime Numbers

Eulcid was Greek mathematician that is well-known for writing the Elements, a collection of books that detail subjects such as geometry and number theory. Did you know that he also provided a famous proof of the fact that there are infinitely-many prime numbers?

Recall that a prime number cannot be evenly divided by any positive number except 1 and the prime itself. Prime numbers are of supreme importantance because of the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, which states that every positive integer can be written uniquely (up to order, since multiplication is commutative) as a product of prime numbers. For example, the number 105 can be written as a product of 3, 5, and 7, and the only way to write 105 as a product of prime numbers is to use exactly one 3, exactly one 5, and exactly one 7.

Euclid's proof use the strategy proof by contradiction. It goes like this: assume the opposite of what you want to be true and demonstrate that this assumptions leads in one way or another to a logical contradiction. In his proof, we assume that there are only a finite number of primes. Then we define a carefully chosen number and use it to find a logical impossibility!
